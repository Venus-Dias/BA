About

The analyst who asks the question behind the question

I didn't become a BA because I loved documentation. I became one because I kept noticing the same problem in every organisation I worked in: smart people were building the wrong things precisely. The technical execution was fine. The requirements were wrong.

Over 20 years across contact centres in India, banking floors in Dubai, and consulting rooms in Auckland, I've learned that the hardest part of any project isn't the build. It's the 90 minutes in a workshop when someone finally says what they actually need, and you have to be sharp enough to hear it and precise enough to write it down so a developer can act on it two months later without calling you.

"Good requirements are invisible. Bad ones are the reason projects fail."

My background gives me something most BAs don't have: I've been on the operations side, the data side, the CX side, and the delivery side of the same problems. That means I can write requirements that the business will sign off on and the technical team will actually build to, because I understand what both sides need to feel confident.

Based in Auckland, I'm currently open to senior BA roles, particularly in financial services, digital transformation, and regulated environments where precision and stakeholder credibility really matter.
